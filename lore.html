World Codex: The Axiom of Growth
41
In the world of Aethel, potential is not an abstract concept but a measurable reality. The physical and mental capabilities of all living beings can be quantified through a system of Attributes and Indicators. This is the 'Axiom of Growth', a fundamental law of existence understood by scholars and warriors alike.
I. Core Attributes (The Four Stats)

These are the foundational pillars that determine an individual's innate and developed capabilities.

Strength (STR): A measure of pure physical power. It governs muscular force, lifting capacity, and the impact of physical blows. Crucially, it also contributes to one's constitution, resilience, and ability to endure injury.

Agility (AGI): Represents an individual's speed, reflexes, and coordination. This stat dictates movement speed, reaction time, balance, and accuracy.

Wisdom (WIS): The attribute of control, perception, and mental fortitude. For mages, Wisdom is paramount for mana efficiency, spell control, and mental resistance. For others, it represents intuition and awareness.

Intelligence (INT): The measure of raw magical potential and processing power. It determines the maximum size of one's mana pool and the raw power or complexity of the magic they can wield.

II. Magical Efficacy

Magic is a combination of raw power and refined control.

Intelligence (The Fuel Tank): Determines the raw size of one's internal mana pool‚Äîthe theoretical maximum magical energy they can hold.

Wisdom (The Engine): Governs the control and efficiency with which a mage can draw upon and shape that mana.

Effective Magic: A mage with high INT but low WIS possesses a vast well of chaotic, untamed power. Their spells are inefficient and unstable. Conversely, a mage with high WIS but low INT has a small mana pool but can use it with surgical precision. True power requires a balance of both.

III. The Tier System (A Measure of Mastery)

The Tier system is a practical classification of an individual's mastery, typically awarded by an official body like an adventurer's guild. It is determined by the highest single stat an individual possesses.

Unranked (Stat Average: 1-9): The common populace.

Copper (Highest Stat: 10-19): The entry-level for adventurers and professional soldiers.

Bronze (Highest Stat: 20-29): Seasoned veterans and elite talents.

Silver (Highest Stat: 30-39): Masters of their craft and regional powers.

Gold (Highest Stat: 40-49): Living legends.

Transcendent (Highest Stat: 50+): Beings who have broken mortal limits.

IV. The Nature of Growth ("Leveling")

Growth is a metaphysical process of the soul and body becoming more "dense" with the world's ambient energy. A "Level" is simply a milestone marker for when an individual has absorbed enough energy to manifest a significant, quantifiable increase in their core attributes. A human of average talent gains 2 stat points per milestone. However, this is a rule that is for NPCs, the player is able to control their stats and how it is assigned, for example, the NPCs might have to survive a life and death encounter or reach ephiphany to gain stats and levels, but the player can simply kill enough monsters or achieve an impressive enough feat to clock up experiences and level up to gain stats.

Struggle & Adaptation: Pushing one's limits in a life-or-death situation. The fastest but most dangerous path.

Mastery & Realization: Completing a task that requires immense focus and skill, such as forging a flawless sword or solving a complex arcane theorem.

Maturation & Saturation: The slow, passive absorption of energy over a long life.

V. The Nature of Magic (The Two Paths)

Magic is the art of imposing one's will upon the environment. However, the method of this imposition separates common mages from true masters.

Formulaic Magic (The Common Path): This is the "recipe-based" magic taught in all academies. Mages learn to use specific words and gestures as arcane keys to unlock complex, pre-packaged sequences of will. Casting "Fireball" is not a single act, but an inefficient, multi-step command to create matter, apply telekinesis, sustain the form, and detonate it. This method is safe, reliable, and the foundation of all magic from the Copper to Gold tiers. A Gold-tier mage is simply a master of running these "scripts" on a massive scale.

Conceptual Magic (The True Path): This is a revolutionary and poorly understood school of thought. It is the art of bypassing the formula and imposing one's will directly on a single, fundamental concept of reality. A conceptual mage does not think "create and throw fire"; they think, "that point in space will become hot." By targeting the end result, they eliminate all wasteful intermediate steps, achieving far greater effects with vastly less mana. This is the secret to true mastery and the domain of Transcendent mages, who do not cast "spells" but issue direct, simple commands to the universe.

World Codex: Faiths and the Divine
43
The gods of Aethel are not distant, abstract beings. They are powerful esoteric entities whose strength, and even their ability to manifest in the mortal realm, is directly influenced by the quantity and quality of worship they receive from their followers. The two major human faiths have vastly different approaches to this divine contract.
I. The Ecclesiarchy of the Divine Covenant

Core Tenet: A polytheistic faith centered on a sacred pact, the "Divine Covenant," made between the gods and mortals to protect the world. Its philosophy is egalitarian and protective, encouraging the strong to defend the weak.

The Pantheon: The faith is built on the worship of the great Archangels and the veneration of mortal heroes who have become Ascended Saints.

The Archangels: Michael the Justicar, Raphael the Healer, Gabriel the Messenger, and Uriel the Vindicator. These are the primary deities.

Ascended Saints: Mortal champions‚Äîheroic paladins and selfless priests‚Äîwho perfectly embodied the virtues of an Archangel. Upon death, their faith and deeds elevate them to sainthood, allowing them to act as patrons and intercessors for the living.

Center of Power: The Pontifical States (analogous to Italy), ruled by the High Pontiff from the sacred city of Aetia.

II. The Path of Eternal Truth

Core Tenet: A monotheistic faith centered on the worship of a single, all-powerful, and unknowable Creator. It teaches that its truth is the only truth and that the highest calling is self-sacrifice in service of expanding the faith's influence and bringing the world into alignment with divine will.

Theology: Adherents view the pantheons and saint-worship of the Ecclesiarchy as primitive and heretical dilutions of the one Eternal Truth. Their faith is a complete way of life, demanding submission to divine will in all things.

Center of Power: The Asranid Sultanate.

III. The Nature of Divine Power

The Power of Belief: A god's power is directly proportional to the number of their followers and the fervor of their belief. A widely worshipped god can manifest miracles, empower champions (Paladins), and grant divine magic to their priests.

The Fracturing of Faith: This is why the gods of monstrous races like Orcs and Goblins are typically weaker. Different tribes may worship the same concept (e.g., a God of Slaughter), but they call it by different names and envision it in different ways. This fractures the "pipeline" of esoteric energy, preventing the god from forming a consistent, powerful consciousness. The unified, dogmatic faiths of humanity are far more effective at empowering their chosen deities.

IV. The Law of Mortal Resonance

The Echo of Champions: The world exists in a state of grim, metaphysical balance. When a great mortal champion‚Äîa Gold-tier hero, a living Paragon, or a Saint‚Äîis slain, their immense potential does not simply vanish. It returns to the world as a form of resonant esoteric energy, an "echo" of their greatness.

The Bloom of Heroes: This echo saturates the mortal realm, dramatically increasing the probability of individuals with extraordinary potential being born or "awakening" to their power in the following years. The death of one great hero directly seeds the birth of the next generation. This is the primary reason humanity, despite its short lifespan and endless wars, is never truly extinguished. Nature always returns what it takes away.

The Eternal War: This is not a benevolent cycle. The Law of Mortal Resonance does not guarantee victory or peace; it only guarantees that the struggle will continue. It ensures that for every monstrous threat that rises, a hero will eventually be born to meet it. This creates a self-perpetuating, eternal conflict, a grinding stalemate that provides a constant source of struggle, faith, and desperation‚Äîthe very things that fuel the gods themselves.

V. The Geopolitical Stalemate (The Eternal Chess Game)

The Consequence of a Grim World: The "Eternal War" is not a state of constant, mindless battle on all fronts. It is a high-stakes game of geopolitical chess played by every ruler in the known world. Every leader is acutely aware that they are beset by multiple threats and that their rivals‚Äîeven their allies‚Äîare constantly watching for a moment of weakness.

A World of Calculated Risks: This situational realism dictates the flow of conflict. A leader who overextends their forces to pursue a victory on one front leaves a fatal opening on another. The Famine Queen cannot throw all her Greenskins south at the humans, for the Dwarves would immediately strike her western flank. The Duke of Normande-Flandria cannot commit his entire fleet to a holy crusade, as the Holy Reich might test his land border. This creates a world of constant, soul-crushing pressure where a true, final victory is almost impossible, as it would create a power vacuum that would immediately be filled by another threat. This ensures the stalemate‚Äîand the gods' source of power‚Äîis preserved.
